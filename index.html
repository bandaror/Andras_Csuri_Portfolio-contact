Intro

I am doing my last year towards my degree in Computing and IT, specialised in software engineering. As my degree is in reach, I am really excited to start working as a junior software developer, and looking forward to learning more and more every day.

Currently I am focusing on c# and the ASP.NET framework using MVC architecture(please see details about my project under the 'Work' section). During my years in the university, I have also studied data science( SQL and MongoDb), web apps, cloud technology, oop principles, java, python, software engineering and many more.

Work

Welder Tracker

This application is developed for a manufacturing company to display the orders for the welders of the cell, and track productivity. Detailed description can be found under the about page of the application. It is published on Azure under a free tier.

About

Here you can read about my experience outside of the IT sector, and a brief journey how I ended up getting my degree.

I got my first job as a metal fabricator in Hungary, and when I arrived in the UK I found a job in a restaurant as a kitchen porter. While I was dealing my own duties I helped out the chefs and it turned out that I have a talent for cooking and performing well under pressure, in a fast paced environment. So much so that I started to jump ranks quickly and became sous chef after a year and a half, then kitchen manager/head chef in the year after. During this time I developed skills that helped me working with other people, how to organise and unite teams in the size of 6-12, how to promote individual efforts. I learned that the best way to lead is by example and that everyone (well, almost) can be motivated with something, and my job was to find that and capitalise in the form of productivity. I gained experience of the financial side of the business as well in the form of stock and budget control. After five years I left London and moved to Dorset hoping for a more quiet life.

I took charge of the kitchen of a Bistro and the owner or the restaurant became a really good friend of mine. As I talked him about my life and my everlong dream to be a software developer, he and his wife encouraged my to take the student loan and start my studies in the IT sector. Two years later they had to go back to Australia, and even though they invited me and my wife we decided to stay. -I was doing my second school year and I really enjoyed it. Strangely enough I realised that I like the struggle I was facing and a feel of success every time I overcame it.

Two years fast forward my daughter born, and it turned out that working long and late hours doesn't go well with a child. I started to work as an assembler first in the aerospace industry, then covid-19 happened and jumped to a manufacturer who is making hospital equipment. Here I used my past experience as a metal fabricator and learned TIG welding. After two years I am almost done with my degree (currently working on my final project) and I feel it is time to start my career in the IT industry as junior software developer. As I was getting closer to reach the end I realised that I need to start focusing on one direction I picked up Visual Studio with c#, and started using ASP.NET framework and the MVC architecture.
